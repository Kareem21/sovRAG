Using Share Structure Explorer

The function can be used to correct details for share transactions as well as rectify errors seen when rebuilding the share register.

Following are examples of scenarios where the function can be used.

  • Share Certificate Number Not Used:

    • Issue with allotment - Subscriber Shareholder status changed.

      The Subscriber Shareholder option previously ticked for a shareholder is un-ticked after performing a share transfer.

      Example:

      1. Issue share to Shareholder A without ticking Subscriber Shareholder option.

      2. Issue share to Shareholder A again, this time, tick the Subscriber Shareholder option.

      3. Transfer shares from Shareholder A to someone else.

      Result – The Subscriber Shareholder option for Shareholder A is now un-ticked (seen as False in the screenshot below).

      Note: For the first and second share issue, consideration per share is the same.

       

      To correct this:

      1. In Shares > Transaction, click Explore.

      2. Edit third and fourth transaction – tick the Subscriber Shareholder option.

    • Issue with redemption - Incorrect Issue Price and Paid.

      Example:

      1. First allotment with paid=1, issue price=1 for Shareholder A

      2. Second allotment with paid=1.1, issue price=1.1 for Shareholder A

      3. Redeem the first allotment with 1000 numbers of shares.

      4. The first allotment share is fully transferred from Shareholder A to Shareholder B

      Result – The paid and issue price mismatch for share redemption and transfer as compared to first allotment.

       

      TO DO: Undo the third transaction and its subsequence transactions and re-enter the same entries

    • Issue when performing call paid - Error seen: Failed to reconcile transaction.

      Example:

      1. First allotment with paid=0.1, issue price=1 for Shareholder A.

      2. The full value of the share is paid by the Shareholder without effective date.

      3. The full value of the share is paid by the Shareholder with effective date.

      4. Post the transaction 2 with any date.

      Result – The share register failed to reconcile after rebuilding shares.

       

       

       

      TO DO: Undo the second and third transactions and fully paid the shares with effective date.

       

    • Issue with forfeiture - Error seen: Problem in shares.

      Example:

      1. First allotment for Shareholder A.

      2. Forfeit 125 shares from Shareholder A.

      Result – The outstanding share is missing (New Certificate is missing to hold the outstanding of the share).

       

       

       

      TO DO: Undo the second transaction and re-enter the same entries.

       

  • Share Certificate Number Used:

    • Issue with allotment - Allotment Transaction is Missing.

      Example:

      Result - Losing track of transaction since no allotment found.

       

       

      TO DO: Undo all orphan transactions and re-enter the same entries.

 

When to edit share transactions and when to reverse and re-enter the transactions?

Edit share transactions using Share Structure Explorer – This can be used to update transaction details such as the transaction date, effective date, share certificate prefix, certificate location, beneficial owner, previous owner, previous certificate number or note.

Reverse and re-enter share transactions – This should be performed to correct share balance, number of shares, transfer total, payment method, and subscriber.
