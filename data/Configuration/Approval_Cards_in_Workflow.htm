Approval Cards in Workflow

Checklist item configured with the Approval Card field type is used to prevent users from going to the next step without approval.

 

Approving a Step

Approving a step can be done in Workflows screen and Workflow Steps screen if the user has access to these screens.

  1. In Viewpoint - Home, go to To Do > Overview > To Do Summary > Jobs – Workflows/Jobs – Workflow Steps or To Do > Action Items > Workflows/Workflow Steps screen.

  2. Select the workflow to update.

  3. Click Open. In the opened workflow, the status and approval Id of the item will be seen.

  4. Click Approval.

  5. The Approval screen is shown on the left panel and the checklist items in the step are shown on the right panel. Users can copy the information in the checklist items and paste them in the Note field in the Approval screen.

  6. Users can view multiple tabs on the right panel if there is a checklist item configured as Go to Screen field type. The information shown can also be copied and pasted in the Note field in the Approval screen.

  7. In the Approval screen, the details of the approval such as the Approval ID, number of approvals required, and users who are authorised to approve will be shown.

  8. Click Approve.

The status of an approved approval card will change from 'Pending Approval' to 'Approved and Completed'.

 

Note: The approval details can also be viewed in the print preview screen of the workflow step containing the approval card by clicking on the ‘Print Workflow’ or ‘Print Step’ ribbon.  

Once a step has been approved, it is considered as completed so the next step becomes the current step.

If the approver does not have access to either the Workflows or Workflow Steps screen, the approving is to be done through Approvals screen.

  1. In Viewpoint - Home, go to To Do > Action Items > Approvals screen.

  2. Select the workflow step to approve.

  3. Click Go to.

  4. In the opened workflow, click Approval to open the Approval screen.

Note: The workflow step may require multiple approvers. In this case, all the approvers must perform the approval before the step is considered as complete.

 

Undoing Step Approval

You can click on ‘Undo Approve’ to undo the approval. This is applicable only when more than one approval is required. After undoing the approval, you will need to re-approve the Approval Card.

 

Rejecting a Step

To reject a workflow step, ensure that you specify the reason in the Note field and then click Reject. This may prompt the New Task screen for a task to be created for the rejection. If so, review t he task details and click Update.

Note: Whether a task is created for rejecting a step or not depends on the configuration of your environment.

 

The rejection task will be placed below the workflow item. If the workflow step is rejected for more than once, the task details of the latest record will be first shown.

Once the task assigned have been completed, you will need to approve the approval card again. In the approval screen, rejection details including timestamp and the approvals are displayed chronologically.  

If there are two or more approval cards within the same workflow step, rejecting a later approval card will void all earlier approvals where the status will change from ‘Approved and Completed’ to ‘Pending Approval’. You will need to approve the approval card again.
