Capital/Income Segregation

This section will show how you can use Viewpoint to do Capital/Income (C/I) segregation in your Client Accounts. C/I is used primarily for Trust Accounts. You need to ensure that the Chart of Accounts template used in the Client Accounts has been marked for C/I segregation; the individual accounts in the Chart of Accounts have been allocated to income, to capital, or to both income and capital; and the transactions entered have been allocated to income or to capital.

 

C/I Segregation - Chart of Accounts Template

In defining the Chart of Accounts template to be used in your Client Accounts, you need to specify whether the C/I segregation will be activated for that Chart of Accounts template.

Note: Once you have activated the C/I segregation in a given Chart of Accounts template, you will not be able to deactivate it.

 

See also:

  • Chart of Accounts - Templates Setup.

 

C/I Segregation - Nominal Accounts

Next you will need to set up the accounts to be used in the Chart of Accounts template you have defined above. In setting up the accounts, you need to decide whether a given account is allocated to income, to capital, or to both income and capital. By default, all posting from Practice Management module will be allocated to Income [I] posting.

If the Account Type is control account, Capital/Income field will default as Both Capital and Income [B].

 

See also:

  • Chart of Accounts - Nominal Accounts Setup.

 

C/I Segregation - Accounting Transactions

If the Account File is using a Chart of Accounts template with C/I segregation, individual accounting transactions created in Viewpoint will need to be allocated to either income or capital. If the transaction involves an allocation to both income and capital, you will need to break down this transaction to the income and capital elements and enter these elements in two separate journal entries.

Once the journal entry is allocated to income or to capital, the accounts used in that journal must follow that allocation. For example, a journal entry allocated to Capital (shown below) can only contain accounts which are allocated to Capital. For such, there is a filtering in the journal:

  • when the selected journal header is Capital, the Account Code will only show accounts where C/I/B is C or B. I is not shown.

  • when the selected journal header is Income, the Account Code will only show accounts where C/I/B is I or B. C is not shown.

C/I Segregation – Reports

The Excel Statements include a Balance Sheet, and Profit and Loss Statement showing this C/I segregation. In addition, you can further customise other Excel Statements to show this C/I segregation. Please refer to the Statement Tutorial on how to customise Excel Statements reports for details.
