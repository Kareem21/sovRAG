Share Split and FIFO Rebalance

The Share Split increases the number of shares in a public company. The price is adjusted such that the before and after market capitalization of the company remains the same and dilution does not occur.

Take, for example, a company with 100 shares of stock priced at $30 per share. The market capitalization is 100 × $30, or $3000. The company splits its stock 2-for-1. There are now 200 shares of stock and each shareholder holds twice as many shares. The price of each share is adjusted to $15. The market capitalization is 200 × $15 = $3000, the same as before the split.

Share Split is now supported for Accounting Securities/Investment Sub-ledgers. Share Split Posting can now apply either Average Book Value or FIFO method.

 

Share Split Posting

To post a Share Split transaction, a single journal line posting is allowed through manual journal creation. As shown below:

 

Field

Description

Movement Type

Select either 'ES' (Securities) or 'IS' (Investment) for the Share Split.

Quantity

Addition/Reduction of shares for the split. Addition Quantity is applicable for Share Split. Reduction Quantity is applicable for Reverse Split.

Please note that the quantity has to be entered manually. For example, if you hold 300 shares and there is a 3 for 1 split, the split adjustment is 600 shares - making the after split quantity balance = 900 shares.

Currency/Amount

Enter '0' zero (There is no change in market capitalisation).

Exchange

Enter '0' zero (There is no change in market capitalisation).

Description

Enter the description for the split. Example: 3-for-1 split, 2-for-1 split.

Line Date

This refers to Journal's line date. This defaults to the day the entry is created.

Reconciliation Date

Enter a reconciliation date for the transaction, if applicable.

Narrative

Enter a narrative for the transaction, if applicable.

 

Click Update when done entering the details. Click Post for the Share Split updates to take effect.

Share Disposal via FIFO Method

In FIFO method, the Price/Share calculation is determined by the quantity of Disposal and Price/Share during the Purchase.

To change to FIFO Method calculation, go to File Maintenance > Accounting > Account File > General. Under Security Valuation field, select FIFO[1].

Security Valuation Screen

The FIFO/LIFO Value section displays the Purchase of securities where balance is NOT NIL. Based on the example of transaction posting given, the share split is reflected in the 'Security Valuation' screen:

 

Average Method:

 

  1. Purchase Quantity: This refers to the quantity purchased.

    • Initial Quantity Purchased: 75.

    • Quantity Purchased after share split on 01/03/2015 (3-for-1 split): 75 x 3 = 225.

  2. Holding Quantity: This refers to the holding quantity (Balance of shares) available as of now.

  3. Price/Share: On 15/01/2015, the original Price/Share was USD 1.80. After the split, the Price/Share is USD 82.14/USD 0.4693714286.

  4. Total: The total amount still holding = Holding Quantity x Price/Share.

 

FIFO Method

  1. Purchase Quantity: This refers to the quantity purchased.

    • Initial Quantity Purchased: 75.

    • Quantity Purchased after share split on 01/03/2015 (3-for-1 split): 75 x 3 = 225.

  2. Holding Quantity: This refers to the holding quantity (Balance of shares) available as of now.

  3. Price/Share: On 15/01/2015, the original Price/Share was USD 1.80. After the split, the Price/Share is USD 0.60.

  4. Total: The total amount still holding = Holding Quantity x Price/Share.
