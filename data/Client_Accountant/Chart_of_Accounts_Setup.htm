Chart of Accounts Setup

There are three layers to the structure of Accounting:

  • Global account classification.

  • Shared templates.

  • Entity accounting.

The global account classification used in Accounting is split into three groups: Main and Sub groups, nominal accounts and sub-ledger accounts. Main and Sub groups are defined globally for the database and can be regarded as a general account classification that pre-defines the reports, balance sheet, profit and loss enquiries. Examples of Main groups are Assets, Liabilities, Expenses and Income.

Each Main group has its own Sub groups, which are linked directly to their corresponding nominal accounts. The global account classification forms the backbone of the program; therefore, it is important to give due weight and consideration when structuring it.

Nominal accounts are either for direct posting, or function as control accounts for sub-ledger accounts. Control accounts have sub-ledger accounts linked to them, where the posting will take place.

For example, a control account can be specified for bank accounts. You would then set up a sub-ledger account for each bank account. Similarly, a control account can be specified for securities, and you would set up separate sub-ledger accounts for each security held. Transactions are recorded in the sub-ledger accounts and posted. Once posted, the control account balances will be updated with the transaction information.

Each grouping of Main, Sub and nominal accounts is known as a Chart of Accounts (CoA) template. You can have as many CoA templates as required, depending on the Account Files that you create in Viewpoint. For example, you may use specific templates for property-holding companies, trade companies and trusts - each template can be used in as many Account Files as required.

A CoA template can be further customised in each individual Account File. Thus, you may use a specific template for a particular entity type, but you may further activate or deactivate nominal accounts in the Account File once the template has been assigned to the Account File.

Journals are used to record transactions into the nominal (general) ledger. There is an optional provisional journal (draft journal) facility for management review and control before permanent posting to the nominal ledger takes place. Account balances are updated after each posting of either a provisional/ draft journal or a regular journal.

The setting up of a Chart of Accounts requires the following to be set up:

  • Main/Sub Groups

  • Templates

  • Nominal Accounts

  • Account Files
