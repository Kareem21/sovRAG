Recurring Fees - Pro-rated Billing

Recurring fees can be pro-rated for fees charged in advance, as well as in arrears. This allows you to begin charging your clients based on the day the service is first provided. The calculation of the amount to charge will take into account:

  • the pro-rate period which is calculated based on the option for Charge Per and dates from the Fee Period From and Fee Period To fields

  • the pro-rate method set in System Defaults > Practice Management, and can be day, week or month

Pro-rated for Recurring Fees in Advance

If a fee of GBP 3000.00 is normally invoiced for 1 January to 31 December, but you now want to raise a pro-rated invoice for the period from 1 April 2021 to 31 December 2021, then the Recurring Fees is to be set up with the following details:

  • Number of Units = 1

  • Unit Price = 3000

  • Discount % = 0.0%

  • Charge Per = Annually

  • Effective Date = 01 April 2021

  • Fee Period From Date = 01 April 2021 (this will be the same as Effective Date)

  • Next Invoicing Due = 01 April 2021

  • Fee Period To Date = 31 December 2021

Note: When setting up the Recurring Fee, the system will automatically set the Fee Period To Date as 31 March 2022, so you need to change this to 31 December 2021.

 

The above will give you the following:

  • Total Period = 01 January 2021 to 31 December 2021

  • Non-Chargeable Period = 01 January 2021 to March 2021

  • Chargeable Period = 01 April 2021 to 31 December 2021

Pro-rata Method set to Day [D]

When the Pro-rata Method is set to Day:

  • Total Period from 1 January 2021 to 31 December 2021 = 365 days

  • Non-Chargeable Period from 1 January 2021 to 31 March 2021 = 90 days
